Question

What is the difference between `let`, `const`, and `var` in JavaScript, and when should you use each?

Answer

`let` and `const` are block-scoped and don't allow redeclaration. `var` is function-scoped and allows redeclaration. Use `const` for constants, `let` for variables that change, avoid `var` in modern JavaScript.

Explanation

Block scope means the variable is only accessible within the nearest enclosing block (between {}). Function scope means accessible throughout the entire function. Hoisting behavior also differs - let/const have temporal dead zones.
